Beddinghouse - End-to-End ERP Modernization

Consumer Goods S/4HANA Cloud Public Oct 2025 - Oct 2026

Organization Introduction

Beddinghouse is a Dutch family business with decades of experience in bed and bath textiles. From their headquarters in Lisse, they design duvet covers, pillowcases, towels, kimonos, and related products. Production takes place entirely with external suppliers‚Äîprimarily in Pakistan‚Äîwhere their own staff supervise quality and compliance with social and ethical standards.

The company operates in both B2C markets (via their own webshop and major platforms like Bol.com and Amazon) and B2B sectors, creating designs for leading brands such as VT Wonen and Oilily. They're also experimenting with new business models, including a consignment pilot at 130 Beter Bed stores and a circular concept called Circular Dreams for returned and second-chance items.

Beddinghouse currently generates approximately ‚Ç¨20 million in revenue with ambitions to grow to ‚Ç¨40 million within five to ten years. The organization has about fifty employees and is actively looking to expand warehouse capacity to facilitate logistical growth.

How We Got This Assignment

The selection of a new ERP system was guided by Skopos, an external party that supports companies with package and partner selections. During this process, multiple vendors and implementation partners were considered. Scheer Nederland was ultimately selected for its business-like, honest working style and strong content-focused chemistry during presales sessions.

Skopos valued the pragmatic and end-to-end process-oriented approach that characterizes Scheer. While other parties presented primarily module-oriented solutions, Scheer emphasized from the outset the coherence between processes, future-proofing, and standardization. This closely aligned with Beddinghouse's wishes to move away from customization and fragmented solutions. The personal connection played a major role: no slick sales pitches, but clear language, realistic expectations, and respect for the SME character of the customer.

Bol.com

E-Commerce Platform | Finance Transformation | 2.5M Monthly Transactions

E-Commerce - Marketplace

Bol.com - Enterprise Finance Modernization at Scale

E-Commerce S/4HANA Cloud Public - Finance 2023 - 2024

Organization Introduction

Bol.com has grown into one of the largest e-commerce platforms in the Netherlands and Belgium, now employing more than 5,000 people with a strong digital DNA. The organization is young, dynamic, and strongly focused on rapid innovation. The company operates in seventy self-managing teams, each responsible for their own development lines and tooling.

This structure offered maximum agility for years but ultimately led to a landscape with many loose systems, limited documentation, and high dependency on individual knowledge that regularly disappeared due to significant internal turnover.

Bol.com has two types of business relationships: suppliers and parties that pay to increase their visibility through advertising services. Therefore, the same party can sometimes be both supplier and customer simultaneously.

The old financial system could no longer keep up with this complexity. The need to modernize the administration was critical, as it forms the heart of all statutory reporting, VAT returns, consolidation, and financial accountability to Ahold Delhaize (parent company).

How We Got This Assignment

Scheer came into the picture after Bol.com concluded that previous conversations with other parties provided insufficient direction. Richard Nagel brought Scheer back into focus, leading to a substantive conversation where it became clear that Bol.com no longer needed a selection process; the choice for SAP had already been made. What was missing was a party that could clearly explain what SAP S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition can and cannot do, and how Bol.com could best deploy it.

Initial conversations took place from summer 2023. The project officially started in early 2024. The complexity became increasingly clear, and Scheer quickly gained sufficient trust to lead the entire implementation.

Team Composition

Scheer Team:

  • Peggy Verkleij & Pim van der Zee - Finance Consultants (Core team)
  • Marina Hommersom - Project Management
  • Barclay Roes - Business Intelligence
  • Mark van Schaik & Sanne van Bruinsvoort - Migrations/Testing
  • Stefanie van Hegelsom-Schulkes - Integrations

Customer Team:

  • Dozens of employees from finance, controlling, operations, procurement, customer service, and integration
  • Challenge: High internal turnover - more than half the key users were replaced during the project

Partners: Minimal external IT partner involvement. Integrations almost exclusively realized by Bol.com's internal development teams, as virtually all systems are built in-house.

Key Challenges

  • System Fragmentation: Enormous number of systems within Bol.com with absence of consistent documentation
  • Knowledge Retention: Many processes grew historically without central governance; knowledge resided with people who often stayed only 1-2 years in a position
  • Legacy Finance System: Old system on the verge of collapse with virtually no future value
  • Organizational Impact: Extremely high impact across entire organization as virtually all transactions from all systems ultimately end up in finance
  • Green Field Finance: Complete absence of structure in general ledger, reporting lines, reconciliation logic, and accounting principles requiring finance to be built from zero
  • Security Requirements: Strict security organization requiring extensive attention to SAP integrations, authentication, whitelisting, and API connections
  • Integration Complexity: Had to analyze and reinterpret dozens of data streams that touched all surrounding systems

Solution & Phases

Implementation: Complete finance restructuring in SAP S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition with focus on Finance, GL structures, Asset Accounting, Payment Processing, and Reporting. Limited Sales Billing configured for specific invoicing like gift cards.

Approach: General ledger structure, reporting model, cost centers, and complete accounting logic were rebuilt from scratch. For virtually every transaction stream, a "recipe book" was developed specifying exactly how data should be interpreted and posted. These recipes now form the basis for integration, maintenance, and management.

Scope Evolution: Project started with small finance scope but due to complexity grew into organization-wide restructuring of all feeding systems.

Integration Landscape

Bol.com processes approximately 2.5 million financial transactions per month from numerous internal systems. Integrations form an extremely extensive landscape including:

  • Webshop sales
  • Supplier costs
  • Advertising services
  • Logistics
  • Customer service
  • Gift cards
  • Payment processing

Virtually everything runs through proprietary middleware that collects, enriches, and forwards data to SAP. These integrations had to be aligned with the new structure and strict security requirements.

SAP BTP Considerations

Due to security requirements, Bol.com could not use all standard BTP services. Authentication, token rotation, and API security required specific solutions. Whitelisting and communication via Google platforms played an important role. BTP deployment was kept functionally limited due to Bol.com's security team requirements.

Expected Benefits

  • Fully standardized financial administration with clear reporting lines and solid governance foundation
  • Uniform posting processing with automatic exception handling
  • Significantly improved audit trail
  • Easier to grow, connect new entities, and support future expansions
  • Faster reporting and better compliance with internal and external control requirements
  • Drastically reduced manual work volume - virtually all transactions now processed automatically
  • Despite enormous data volume, error rate significantly reduced and processes better manageable

Lessons Learned

  • Mature Project Structure Required: Projects of this scale require mature project structure from day one. Absence of strong customer-side project manager caused early delays and ambiguity
  • Executive Sponsorship Critical: When Bol.com senior management recognized project was business-critical, governance improved and collaboration strengthened significantly
  • Knowledge Documentation Essential: Organizations with high internal turnover must invest heavily in documentation and knowledge transfer systems
  • Recipe Books Work: Creating detailed transaction "recipe books" proved invaluable for integration consistency and long-term maintainability
  • Security Early: Security requirements should be addressed at project start, not during integration phase
  • Scope Discipline: In complex organizations, maintaining finance-only scope while resisting pressure to expand requires strong governance

Reference Hooks

  • E-commerce platforms with high transaction volumes
  • Finance-only implementations in complex IT landscapes
  • Organizations with 70+ self-managing development teams
  • High employee turnover environments
  • Strict security and compliance requirements
  • 2.5M+ monthly financial transactions
  • Dual supplier-customer relationships
  • Green-field GL and accounting structure design
  • Recipe-based integration approach
  • Parent company consolidation requirements (Ahold Delhaize)

Organization Introduction

FineField is an innovative manufacturer focused on harvesting machines for blueberries. The company originated from the Driessen brothers in 2012 and has grown from a pioneering startup to a recognized player in agricultural technology. Their robotic harvesters use advanced vision systems and AI to autonomously pick blueberries with speed and care that match or exceed manual labor.

FineField operates with approximately 25-30 employees and generates estimated revenue of ‚Ç¨10-15 million. The company is in strong growth mode, expanding from the Netherlands to international markets including North America. They combine machine manufacturing with ongoing service contracts, spare parts supply, and machine upgrades.

The business model includes selling machines outright, leasing arrangements, and service-based models where FineField maintains ownership and charges per harvested kilogram. This creates complex requirements for project-based manufacturing, asset management, and service logistics.

How We Got This Assignment

FineField was operating with basic accounting software that could no longer support their growth and operational complexity. They needed integrated manufacturing, project management, and service capabilities. After evaluating several options, they concluded that cloud ERP was necessary to support international expansion without building internal IT infrastructure.

Scheer was introduced through the SAP partner network and stood out for understanding both manufacturing complexity and the unique requirements of robotics companies that combine product sales with ongoing service operations. The fit-to-standard message resonated because FineField wanted proven processes, not custom development experiments.

  • Business Model Variations: Supporting direct sales, leasing, and usage-based service models simultaneously

The Ocean Cleanup

Non-Profit Organization | Ocean Plastic Removal | Global Operations

NGO - Environmental Technology

The Ocean Cleanup - ERP Foundation for Global Mission

NGO / Environmental S/4HANA Cloud Public 2024 - Phase 1 Complete

Organization Introduction

The Ocean Cleanup is a Dutch non-profit organization dedicated to removing plastic pollution from oceans and rivers worldwide. Founded by Dutch inventor Boyan Slat, it operates from Rotterdam with several hundred employees and volunteers globally. The headquarters is located on Coolsingel in a building that looks like a squat from outside but is fully modernized inside as a modern office.

The organization is internationally oriented, multicultural, and has a young, idealistic team with relatively high turnover. Besides the Rotterdam headquarters, The Ocean Cleanup has multiple legal entities worldwide used for funds, local activities, and asset management. They operate both ocean systems and river solutions (Interceptors), with different product lines housed in different company codes for legal and financial structure reasons.

How We Got This Assignment

The implementation assignment came to Scheer via SAP, who positioned Scheer as a suitable implementation partner. The organization was moving away from NetSuite, which was insufficiently integrated for their growing and complex activities. NetSuite offered primarily purchasing and finance but lacked full inventory management or project insight. Transitioning to integrated ERP became necessary, and they sought a partner to help professionalize step-by-step and move away from scattered Excel lists.

Team Composition

Scheer Team:

  • Axel - Project Lead
  • Gerrit - Finance
  • Pieter - Procurement
  • Michael - Technical adjustments support

Customer Team: Approximately 5 people - two from Procurement, two from IT, one from Finance. High employee turnover meant frequent onboarding of new team members, making the project less predictable and requiring Scheer team flexibility.

Key Challenges

  • Process Immaturity: Little to no standardized processes; much work done manually in Excel
  • No Inventory Management: Lacked inventory control, minimal master data usage, limited structure in procurement and finance
  • Organizational Structure: Not strongly structured; reactive and ad-hoc working style
  • ERP Inexperience: Team had virtually no ERP experience
  • High Turnover: Frequent staff changes made knowledge building difficult
  • Unclear Decision-Making: Sometimes ambiguous approval processes and planning communication
  • Limited Visibility: Existing systems provided insufficient insight into costs, projects, inventory, and operations
  • Distributed Assets: Inventory spread across external warehouses with no visual tracking of components
  • Global Pressure: Under worldwide pressure to work efficiently due to donations, subsidies, and societal expectations

Solution & Phases

Phase 1 (Complete): Simple Source-to-Pay scope including Finance for accounts payable. Deliberately chose very limited first step without inventory management or extensive integrations. Non-stock materials and service procurement where orders are directly posted to projects or cost objects.

Phase 2 (In Progress): Collection of smaller enhancements focused on usability and automation:

  • Automatic emailing of purchase orders to suppliers
  • Uploading data from contract management system
  • Effectiveness improvements within existing scope

Phase 3 (Planned January): Major step toward inventory management, warehouse structure, and possibly sales orders. Multiple warehouses in Netherlands, primarily with external logistics partners. Goal: Make components, spare parts, and operational supplies visible in SAP. Expected to bring largest substantive change including process and role impacts.

SAP Solutions

  • SAP S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition - Source-to-Pay and Finance focus
  • SAP Central Invoice Management (CIM) for automatic invoice processing and matching to purchase orders
  • Planned: Inventory Management and Warehouse Management for Phase 3

Integration Considerations

Phase 1 had no external integrations. Phase 2 may import data from contract management tool (scope unclear). Future: Inventory management combined with warehouse systems and project planning may lead to integration requirements, but not yet detailed.

SAP BTP

No significant BTP solutions deployed. Some forms adjusted within standard S/4HANA functionality (not via BTP). Future phases may benefit from BTP extensions, especially around warehouse processes, mobile apps, or operational system connections, but no decisions made yet.

Expected Benefits

While direct benefits remain limited due to small first phase scope, the implementation provides an important foundation:

  • Digital core with real-time insight and growth capability
  • Uniform system helps track costs per project
  • More professional management of donations and funding streams
  • Future: Extensive reporting, inventory management, and worldwide support
  • Strategic Value: Scalable foundation for further process professionalization

Lessons Learned

  • Flexibility Required: Project demanded much flexibility due to low client organizational structure and process maturity
  • Staff Turnover Impact: Employee turnover made knowledge retention difficult
  • Small Steps Work: Concrete, incremental steps work best in environments where ERP is new
  • Early Role Clarity: Important to invest significant energy early in clarifying roles, responsibilities, and expectations
  • NGO Differences: Non-profit organizations require different change management approaches than commercial enterprises
